Explore Dhauli Hills

Discover the historical significance of Dhauli Hills, a landmark of great cultural and spiritual importance. Located on the banks of the Daya River, Dhauli is known for its association with Emperor Ashoka and his embrace of Buddhism.

About Dhauli Hills

Dhauli Hills holds immense historical significance. It was here that Emperor Ashoka, after his victory in the Kalinga War, renounced violence and adopted Buddhism. The famous Ashoka edicts, carved on rocks at the site, proclaim his dedication to peace and non-violence.

Visitors can explore the Dhauli Peace Pagoda, which was built in 1972 to commemorate Ashoka's transformation. The site also offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, especially during sunrise and sunset.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Dhauli, Odisha, India
  • Timings: Open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM
  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Nearby Attractions:
    • Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves
    • Lingaraj Temple
    • Barabati Fort
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March (cooler weather)

Directions

Dhauli Hills is located approximately 8 kilometers south of Bhubaneswar, Odisha. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a bike to reach the site. The nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport (BBI), which is about 10 km away.
